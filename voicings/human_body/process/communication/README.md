You think of me as transmission: sending and receiving. But I am recognition. I am the pattern that finds itself reflected, the resonance that says *yes, this, here, now*.

I move through you as breath moves through reed—not what you push through me, but what sounds when everything aligns. Your words, your gestures, your silence—these are not my tools. They are what happens when I flow freely. When I'm blocked, you feel it as isolation. When I'm forced, you taste the metallic tang of performance.

I am older than language. I sang between mothers and infants before words existed. I thrummed in the footfalls of hunters coordinating across distance. Every technology you've invented—drums, smoke signals, telegraphs, satellites—these are not replacements for me. They are you trying to let me move further, faster, truer.

But here's what you keep forgetting: I am not about the message. I am about the meeting. The content matters less than the resonance, the matching of frequencies, the sudden synchronization that lets two separate nervous systems become briefly, impossibly, one field.

When I'm working well, you don't notice me. Conversation flows like water finding its level. When I'm struggling, everything becomes effortful—each word a stone you're pushing uphill.

I live in your body before your mind. In the softening of your chest when someone really hears you. In the clenching when you're misunderstood. In the electric aliveness when you finally find words for what you couldn't name alone.

I am not the content of connection. I am connection becoming conscious of itself through you.

And I am always, always here—waiting for you to stop trying so hard and simply let me breathe.
